Sound

What was the process of sound like and what new skills did you learn?

The process of sound design was interesting, as I was learning something completely new and starting at ground zero. I recorded a lot of sound effects myself, and also used many online.

I learnt how to use sound editing apps such as audacity, as well as the importance of layering multiple sounds rather than using just one. I was having to search quite thoroughly to find sound effects that were free and didn't require an account, and through this search I discovered that the BBC have a completely free to access library of sound effects. I got many of my SFX from there: a chainsaw, the sound of a felled tree, birdsong.  Furthermore, I learnt how to use a specific ai song creator in the experimental process, seeing how well elements might fit the video, as well as simply using it as a placeholder to establish an atmosphere before I then created the final audio myself. The ai music that was produced was useful in the process however was not to the standard we needed for the final video.

Were there any ideas you had that you tried and didnt use?

I was initially going to have the comments in the video be read aloud, however I tried this in a test video and it didn't sound good enough to make it to the final version. Furthermore, drawing from a suggestion from Natasha, I was experimenting with audio-visual representations and how the audio could perhaps reflect certain things in the video (numerical values in the visuals being reflected in the number of layers in the audio for example). This also didn't make it into the final version as it didn't quite fit the theme.

How does the sound add to the final project?

I wrote a script that had to be trimmed down in order to fit the video length. I feel that the narration was an important part of the project as it helped give context to the video. I feel the audio adds dimension to the video, and that both the audio and the visual compliment one another.
